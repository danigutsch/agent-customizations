# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

This repository aims to be welcoming, respectful, and constructive for everyone who participates.

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and patience
- giving and receiving feedback respectfully
- assuming good intent while discussing technical disagreements
- focusing criticism on ideas, implementations, and tradeoffs rather than people
- helping keep discussions relevant, inclusive, and collaborative

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, threats, or discriminatory language
- personal attacks, insults, or trolling
- publishing private information without permission
- sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or community spaces
- any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to repository spaces and project interactions, including issues, pull
requests, discussions, code review, and other communication channels maintained for this project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it privately to
`daniutsch@outlook.com`.

Please include as much relevant context as you can, such as links, screenshots, timestamps, or a
summary of what happened. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as
practical.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other
contributions that do not align with this code of conduct. They may also take any other action they
consider appropriate for protecting participants and the project.
